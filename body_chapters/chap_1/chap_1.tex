\section{Problem Statement}

Unit tests are the gold standard to test programs to catch analysis or programming errors. An ideal unit test only assesses one behaviour of the program, is repeatable and requires no software engineer intervention. They often aid in the debugging process, allowing software engineers to identify bugs faster, and earlier in the product life cycle. However, the creation of unit tests is often falsely perceived to not contribute to the productâ€™s development. Creating these tests also requires effort to gather the inputs to the test, and requires the software engineer to create test oracles (assertions in JUnit style testing frameworks) to model correct behaviour. As programs evolve, these oracles may shift, and inputs to the test may also change.
